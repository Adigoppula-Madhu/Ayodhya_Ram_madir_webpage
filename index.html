Introduction to Ayodhya Ram Mandir

The Ayodhya Ram Mandir, also known as the Ram Janmabhoomi temple, is a significant religious site located in Uttar Pradesh, India. It holds immense cultural and historical importance in Hinduism and has been a center of both religious devotion and political controversy. more..

Historical Background of Ayodhya

Early Origin

Ayodhya is one of the seven sacred cities in Hinduism and is believed to be the birthplace of Lord Rama. It has a rich and storied history dating back thousands of years.

Royal Kingdom

Ayodhya served as the capital of the ancient kingdom of Kosala and is mentioned in Hindu epics such as the Ramayana.

Cultural Heritage

The city is steeped in tradition, folklore, and spirituality, making it a revered pilgrimage site for millions of devotees.

Significance of Lord Ram in Hinduism

Divine Avatar

Lord Ram is revered as the seventh avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu, symbolizing virtues such as righteousness, morality, and compassion.

Epic of Ramayana

The epic details the life and adventures of Lord Ram and is considered a sacred scripture in Hinduism, teaching valuable life lessons.

Cultural Inspirations

Lord Ram's teachings and actions continue to guide and inspire people across various aspects of life, from morality to governance.

Bala-rama

Bala-rama, also called Halayudha, as the elder brother of Krishna both of whom appear in the legends of Hinduism, Buddhism and Jainism.

Legal Battle and Court Verdict

Land Dispute

Years of legal proceedings led to a landmark verdict by the Supreme Court of India, effectively ending the contentious legal battle over the ownership of the site.

Ruling and Resolution

The court's decision allowed for the construction of the Ram Mandir and the allocation of an alternate site for the construction of a mosque.

Man behind Ram Mandir and consecration ceremony

Keshava Parasaran (born 9 October 1927) is a lawyer from India. An eminent Hindu scholar and an excellent government lawyer, Parasaran has enjoyed the confidence of every administration since the 1970s. His court speeches are often lectures on Hindu scriptures. Not only this, Supreme Court Justice (retd) Sanjay Kishan Kaul had given Parasaran the title of “Father of the Indian Bar for his contribution to law without compromising his religion”.
